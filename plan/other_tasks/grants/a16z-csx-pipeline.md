### Section 2: What does AgentTrust do? (target ~400 words)

AgentTrust is the canonical Know-Your-Agent (KYA) implementation on Solana. Three Anchor programs that complete the Foundation's ERC-8004 trust stack:

1. **PolicyVault** — Anchor program with five composable policy kinds (Spending limits, CounterpartyTier verification, Velocity ledger, RequireValidation gating, KillSwitch multisig pause) + `gate_payment` composer instruction. Manual cross-program PDA deserialization to Quantu's `agent-registry-8004` and `atom-engine` lets PolicyVault read counterparty trust tiers, risk scores, and reputation history without needing a parallel registry. Five Kani formal-verification invariants prove correctness of the entire decision logic via `cargo kani` in CI.

2. **TrustGate** — Anchor program (PDA-signed `give_feedback` CPI to agent-registry-8004) + TypeScript Express service implementing x402 spec verbatim (`/verify` `/settle` `/dispute` `/receipt` endpoints) + drop-in TS module `@agenttrust/trustgate` with `mountTrustGate(app, config)` so any Express x402 facilitator integrates AgentTrust in 1 line.

3. **ValidationRegistry** — the third leg of ERC-8004 that Quantu archived in v0.5.0, fully productized. Six instructions (`request_validation` / `respond_to_validation` / `revoke_validation` / `read_attestation` / `register_attestor` / `register_namespace`). 10 v1 capability namespaces (KYC tiers, smart-contract audits, model cards, jurisdiction compliance, agent provenance). Permissionless attestor model with downstream-consumer-filtering sybil resistance.

The use case anchor: Solana processed fifteen million agent-driven payments last quarter. As volume rose, so did counterparty fraud — last week a treasury bot routed $1.2M USDC to a clone of a real Solana protocol. Smart contracts held up; the human-trust layer didn't. There's no on-chain check that gates payments on counterparty identity and reputation against the registry Solana Foundation just endorsed. AgentTrust IS that check. (Variant B per `plan/final_idea/PITCH_FRAMES_LOCKED.md`.)

KYA-stack alignment: PolicyVault's CounterpartyTier policy = Identity-tier-aware permission layer. ValidationRegistry's capability namespaces (kyc.tier-1, model-card, jurisdiction, audit.smart-contract) = the cryptographic claims that make agent identities verifiable. TrustGate's PDA-signed feedback emission = the reputation-history primitive that makes counterparty trust meaningful over time. Together: the KYA stack a16z's 2026 thesis explicitly calls for, but on Solana (where Foundation has endorsed the underlying agent registry).

### Section 3: Why does this need to exist? (target ~250 words)

a16z's 2026 thesis frames it directly: *"non-human identities reaching 96 times that of human employees, but these identities are still ghosts unable to access banking systems."* Today's AI agents on x402 cannot verify counterparty identity, reputation, or capability claims at the protocol level. They can settle payments via x402, but they cannot ANSWER "should I pay this counterparty?" at the cryptographic-trust layer.

The gap is acknowledged at the protocol level:
- ERC-8004 (Ethereum's standard) defines Identity, Reputation, AND Validation registries — but most EVM implementations ship only Identity + Reputation
- Quantu shipped Identity + Reputation on Solana via `agent-registry-8004` (Foundation-endorsed); archived the Validation registry in v0.5.0
- Solana Foundation joined Linux Foundation's x402 Foundation in April 2026
- 35M+ x402 transactions processed on Solana by March 2026 ($600M annualized; 65% of all x402 transactions)
- Galaxy Digital forecasts 5% of Solana transactions on x402 by end-of-2026
- a16z forecasts $30 trillion x402 market over 5 years

The Validation registry gap is the specific, named, Foundation-acknowledged gap that AgentTrust's third component fills. PolicyVault + TrustGate complete the consumption layer (how facilitators integrate). ValidationRegistry completes the protocol layer (the third ERC-8004 leg). Together: KYA on Solana, productized, Foundation-aligned, MIT-licensed.

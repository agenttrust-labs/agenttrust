### A.2 Cross-cycle structural patterns (synthesis)

Patterns that hold across all 8 winners' inferred / disclosed structure:

1. **Hook 5-10 seconds, never longer.** Per Colosseum guidance: "Be concise when telling your product story." Per YC: "lead with whatever is most impressive about your company, not what comes first in a traditional pitch deck template." [Source: [ycombinator.com/blog/guide-to-demo-day-pitches/](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/guide-to-demo-day-pitches/)] Mohit's Variant B opener at 30s is *intentionally longer* because it carries problem + Foundation-alignment in one beat — the trade is justified by the premium of the Foundation reference, but it's a deliberate variance from the 5-10s norm.

2. **Demo placement is mid-video (between minute 1:00 and 2:00) for 8/8 winners.** Demo is not the lead and not the close. It's the proof embedded between narrative beats.

3. **Voiceover dominates over on-camera founder for 7/8 winners.** Only Ore + Reflect lean heavily on founder-on-camera. Voiceover lets the screen content be the visual — important for technical demos where the CLI is the visual.

4. **Founder appears on camera at close for 6/8 winners.** Even voiceover-dominant pitches put the founder on screen at the end. The founder authority moment is visual — judges pattern-match on "who is this person."

5. **Music behavior consistent:** absent or near-absent during opener (problem-landing); builds during solution; peaks at demo or end-card; restrained in CLI / metric-heavy moments. Per `friend-handoff-brief.md`, this is the music spec applied to AgentTrust.

6. **Editing pace varies by content density.** Demo / UI heavy = fast cuts. Architecture / vision = slow cuts. Mohit's technical demo follows the fast-cut pattern (CLI-dense); pitch video follows the slow-cut pattern (narrative-led).

7. **Numbers / metrics on screen, not just spoken.** Reflect, Yumi, Capitola — all surface metrics visually (TAM number, transaction count, growth %). Per YC: "the traction slide is the most important element... choose the single metric that makes the company look most impressive and build the entire slide around it." [Source: [ycombinator.com/blog/guide-to-demo-day-pitches/](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/guide-to-demo-day-pitches/)] AgentTrust's load-bearing on-screen numbers: 5 invariants proven, $50M monthly x402 volume, 65% Solana share, 17 days, 99.99% (Vibhu's claim).

8. **Foundation alignment / "why Solana" baked in for 6/8 winners.** Reflect, Ore, MCPay, TapeDrive, Capitola, Samui all surface a "why Solana" beat. AgentTrust's Foundation-endorsement reference IS the why-Solana beat — strongest possible form.

### A.3 What does NOT show up in the pattern table (anti-pattern signal)

- **No comedy openers.** Hackathon winners do not open with jokes. Direct, problem-anchored, fact-led.
- **No dramatic music drops.** None of the 8 use trailer-style epic builds. Subtle, tech-aesthetic.
- **No founder-team-introduction in first 30 seconds.** Team intro is mid-video or close, never opener. (Y Combinator guide: "If you're going to introduce yourself, do it briefly at the start... but the lead is the company.")
- **No animated logo flourishes longer than 1 second.** Title cards exist but stay <2s.
- **No over-claim language.** Per Matty: "Trustless, decentralized bridges do not exist. And probably never will exist." [[2026-04-20](https://x.com/mattytay/status/2046021326683734378)]. AgentTrust's pitch avoids "the only," "revolutionary," "paradigm shift" per same anti-over-claim rule.

## B. Pitch deck patterns (YC + Solana hackathon hybrid)

### B.1 YC standard 10-slide structure

Per [ycombinator.com/blog/how-to-design-a-better-pitch-deck/](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/how-to-design-a-better-pitch-deck/) + [ycombinator.com/library/2u-how-to-build-your-seed-round-pitch-deck](https://www.ycombinator.com/library/2u-how-to-build-your-seed-round-pitch-deck):

| Slide | Purpose | YC quote (≤15 words) |
|-------|---------|----------------------|
| 1 | Title | "Start with your company name and a simple one-liner that explains what you do." |
| 2 | Problem | "State the problem in one sentence that investors immediately understand." |
| 3 | Solution | "Clearly articulate how your product or service solves the identified problem." |
| 4 | Market | "Outline the size and potential of your target market." |
| 5 | Business Model | "Clarifying how your startup generates revenue." |
| 6 | Traction | "Evidence of your startup's market acceptance and growth potential." |
| 7 | Competition | "Information about your competitive landscape." |
| 8 | Team | "Why you are uniquely positioned to solve this problem." |
| 9 | Financials | "Share your financial projections and unit economics." |
| 10 | Ask / Closing | "Explain your fundraising plan. Tie the funding to clear milestones." |

### B.2 Hackathon-specific adaptations (vs YC seed-round deck)

Per Colosseum's `how-to-win` guide + 8-winner observation:

| YC slide | Hackathon adaptation | Why it differs |
|----------|----------------------|----------------|
| Title | Hook (problem-anchored, like opener) | Hackathon judges have 1-3 minutes per submission; hook leads, not name |
| Problem | Same | — |
| Solution | Same — but with concrete demo screenshot | Hackathon = "show working demo on devnet/mainnet" |
| Market | Compressed — one beat with one stat | Smaller TAM proof needed than seed-round; market sniff-test only |
| Business Model | **Replaced with Architecture / How-It-Works** | Hackathon judges weigh technical proof higher than monetization plan in first round; business model only appears for accelerator interviews |
| Traction | **Replaced with Foundation / why-Solana alignment** | At submission day, hackathon teams have <30 days of "traction"; hackathon-specific equivalent is alignment with Foundation-endorsed narrative |
| Competition | **Often omitted** | Hackathon judges already know the competitive landscape; saying "we beat X" reads as defensive — better to claim a clean wedge |
| Team | Same | — |
| Financials | **Replaced with Roadmap (Phase 1 / 2 / 3)** | Hackathon = "do you have a credible post-hackathon plan"; financial projections premature |
| Ask | Hackathon-specific asks (Standout, Cohort 5, Public Goods, grant pathway) | Each ask maps to a different prize/program axis the hackathon offers |

## C. Technical demo video patterns

### C.1 Format choice matrix (live CLI vs pre-recorded vs hybrid)

| Format | Pros | Cons | Recommended for |
|--------|------|------|-----------------|
| **Live CLI** (real-time terminal record) | Authentic, judge-trustable, shows actual mainnet program logs | Brittle (RPC failures, network lag); no second take inside the take | Sub-30-second moments only |
| **Pre-recorded screen capture** | Clean, polished, can re-do | Risks "this looks staged" suspicion; reduced authenticity | Architecture diagrams, flow visualizations |
| **Hybrid** (CLI + voiceover, edited together) | Best of both — authenticity + polish | Requires post-production discipline | **Recommended for AgentTrust technical demo** |

### C.2 The Instagram-worthy moment pattern

Per `judges-and-bias.md` and `agenttrust-pitch-compression.md`: every winning hackathon technical demo has *one screen-shareable moment* a Twitter user can frame and post.

Examples from the 8-winner sample:
- **Unruggable:** the air-gap setup completing in <30 seconds (visual: phone + hardware wallet handshake animation)
- **Reflect:** the delta-neutral yield calculator showing real-time hedge pairing
- **TapeDrive:** the cost comparison overlay (1,400× cheaper) appearing live

**AgentTrust's Instagram-worthy moment:** `cargo kani` running with five green checks for the five invariants appearing in sequence. Per `technical-demo-script.md` Section "Optional 30-second extension." This frame is the standalone Twitter-shareable image (referenced in `twitter-thread-demo-preview.md` Tweet 3).

### C.3 Common technical demo pitfalls (from Colosseum guidance)

Per [blog.colosseum.com/perfecting-your-hackathon-submission/](https://blog.colosseum.com/perfecting-your-hackathon-submission/):

- "Excessive visual effects lacking substance"
- "Overusing industry jargon"
- "Unclear or overly technical descriptions"
- "Failing to clearly articulate core idea and impact"

Per Y Combinator pitch guide:
- "Speaking too quickly without pauses"
- "Excessive movement or pacing"
- "Including slides that don't persuade investors"

**AgentTrust's mitigation in `technical-demo-script.md`:**
- Real terminal output, not animation
- One Instagram-worthy moment (not 5)
- Voiceover paced with deliberate pauses on failure-noun + Foundation-reference
- 90-second base length (well under 2:30 attention threshold)

Pre-decided the day-by-day calendar in §H — don't deliberate cadence under pressure mid-build.

9. **The 4-template Twitter thread system covers the full Day 5 → Day 17 funnel.** Each thread maps to a specific build milestone. Replies between threads sustain daily cadence. No template needs to be re-written — just personalized with mainnet program IDs / facilitator quotes / repo URL when they land.

## G. Cross-cycle observations (raw, synthesis in post-mortem-patterns.md)

- **Stack detail is systematically undisclosed** in official Colosseum announcements. Team size, Anchor-vs-native-Rust, mainnet-vs-devnet — not published.
- **Demo Day cohorts give the clearest signal of which winners "made it."** Cohort 4 (April 15, 2026) included Kormos, Unruggable, Vistadex, Credible, Framesag, Archer, Superfan, GetRekt, YumiFinance, SynthesisTrade, Cloak — ~11 of ~27 Cypherpunk winners. ~40% conversion hackathon-winner → accelerator.
- **The same team can iterate across cycles**: Unruggable placed in Breakout Infra runner-up (2025), then won Cypherpunk Grand (2025/26). Matty explicitly says this is the expected path: "Developers often enter multiple hackathons before winning."
- **Prior cycle Grand Champions frequently reappear as Frontier sponsors**: Reflect (Radar Grand → Frontier sponsor), Vanish (Breakout DeFi track → Frontier $10K side bounty), FluxRPC (Breakout Infra Grand → Frontier builder plan). Winning isn't just the $30K — it's insertion into the Colosseum flywheel.
- **Prize amounts in Frontier are lower than prior cycles** — Grand went from $50K (last 4 cycles) to $30K in Frontier. Offset by removing sponsor-track $25K-30K prizes, so the pool shape shifted: fewer big winners, more medium ($10K × 23) to spread across the top 20. This means the competitive curve is flatter — more projects in the money but per-slot cash is smaller.

## What this means for Mohit's submission

- **Anchor teams and mainnet status are invisible at announcement time.** Do not over-optimize around "judges want native-Rust" or "mainnet is required" — there is zero evidence of either bias in public announcements. Mainnet presence + explicit "why Solana" narrative in the technical demo video matter more than the stack choice per se.
- **Target the Cohort-5-likely shape, not the Grand.** ~40% of cash winners convert to accelerator. The realistic metric is: "would this team be a credible Cohort 5 entrant?" That framing narrows thesis selection — infra primitives, stablecoin infra, and DeFi-with-real-liquidity-story are over-represented in Cohort 4. PFPs/meme/speculative-only are under-represented.
- **Sponsor-alumni-win-again is a template**: Reflect won Radar → sponsors Frontier. Matty rewards teams that *deliver* post-hackathon. A Mohit thesis framed as "this becomes a Frontier-25 sponsor" is calibrated correctly.
- **Iteration is explicitly blessed by the Colosseum founder.** If Mohit has a prior shipped Solana thing even adjacent to the Frontier thesis, leaning into that prior work as founder-market-fit in the pitch video is correct — not cheating. Unruggable did this and it worked.
- **The $10K Standout tier is where the density of realistic slots is** — 20 teams. That's where the probability mass lives for a solo builder. Optimize the submission for "top 20 of ~3,500" (Matty said project creation is pacing at 2.5× prior cycle → ~3,500 final submissions expected) rather than "single #1." The shape of a Standout is: solid working product + clear wedge + credible founder — NOT a world-changing moonshot.
- **Don't build in Consumer track directly** as a solo Mohit. 80%+ of Consumer winners in Cypherpunk/Breakout had strong design/distribution halves of teams — Capitola, Trepa, Superfan, Toaster.trade all present with polished consumer UX. Solo-senior-engineer authority reads better in Infra, DeFi primitive, RWA oracle, Stablecoin infra, or privacy/security primitives — categories where judges trust one deep engineer over a design-led team. This is the single most important vertical-selection heuristic from this file.
